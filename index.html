«He loves the sounds, smells, colors - the diversity of life in the green world around him.»

A disaster is threatening the rainforest, a tragedy that may literally turn the world up-side-down …

About 17 percent of the Amazonian rainforest has been destroyed over the past 50 years, and losses have recently been on the rise - and for Mino, born in the South American rainforest, this threat will have major consequences for his life ...

While he as a young boy preys on the rarest of the beautiful butterflies together with his father, their small community is disturbed by the major petrol companies wanting to exploit the rainforest. One day when returning from his daily hunt, Mino finds his family and friends massacred by the military which are in the pocket of the petrol companies.

«Their love towards life can only be surpassed by the hatred towards the system that killed the ones they loved.»

Later he will get known all over the world as one of the creators of the dangerous Mariposa movement, aiming to direct the world’s attention to the destruction of nature caused by international companies, using the rainforest’s deadliest weapon.

The author

Gert Nygårdshaug was born in Tynset, the mountains of Norway. Since his literary debut in 1966, he is the author of nearly 40 books, both novels for children, poetry and fairytales- together with literary works, thrillers and crime novels.

The books

The trilogy of Mino (Mengele Zoo, The Heaven ́s Flower Tree and The Basin of Aphrodite) earned him immense recognition from critics and the public. Nearly 400,000 copies are sold in Norway, and Mengele Zoo was elected as Norway ́s favorite novel in the Norwegian international literature festival in Lillehammer in 2007.
